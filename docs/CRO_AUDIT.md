## Brand And Offer Extracted

- Brand name: Corbin & Co Personal Real Estate Corporation / Call Corbin Team.
- Main offer: Langley and Fraser Valley real estate help for buying and selling homes.
- Primary audience: homeowners, buyers, first-time buyers, upsizers, downsizers, relocators, and investors across Langley, Cloverdale, Surrey, Clayton Heights, Abbotsford, White Rock, Maple Ridge, and Chilliwack.
- Primary conversion goal: request a home value or contact the team.
- Secondary conversion goals: search listings, browse city guides, read reviews, use resources, call or text.
- Differentiators: 850+ homes sold, 11+ years, no-fluff advice, fast replies, local pricing knowledge, sharp negotiation, professional media, staging guidance, off-market leads when available, city/neighbourhood expertise.
- Trust signals: 200+ 5-star reviews, 211 Google reviews shown on the live homepage, awards/logos, Stonehaus Realty affiliation, Rate-My-Agent presence, Top 1% positioning on the team page.
- Tone of voice: direct, plain-English, confident, local, informal, and slightly no-BS.
- Visual cues: blue social accent, blue/grey brokerage marks, local home photography, realtor/team portraits, review widgets.

## Current CRO Issues

- The first screen leads with "Find out what your home is worth" but the page also speaks to buyers, sellers, investors, and first-time buyers, so intent is slightly blurred.
- Repeated navigation blocks and long review feeds push the clearest conversion paths down the page.
- Strong proof exists but is scattered across homepage, team page, reviews, and contact page.
- The current page relies heavily on embedded widgets and form fields before visitors have a tight reason to convert.
- Buyer and seller journeys are detailed but feel like separate articles rather than clear decision paths.
- The visual system feels less cohesive than the strength of the brand proof.
- Secondary CTAs exist, but they compete instead of creating a simple "not ready yet" path.

## Conversion Strategy

1. Clarify the first-screen promise:
   "Real estate decisions with sharper local advice."

2. Make the primary CTA seller-oriented:
   "Get a 24-hour home value" links to the real home evaluation page.

3. Make the secondary CTA buyer-oriented:
   "Search live listings" links to the live property search.

4. Add a non-pressure path for earlier visitors:
   Resources and city guides become the secondary education path.

5. Bring trust forward:
   850+ sales, 211 live-site Google reviews, 11+ years, local market coverage, fast contact.

6. Reframe the brand promise:
   The site is not just "buy/sell with us"; it is decision protection around price, timing, competition, and negotiation.

7. Keep CTAs honest:
   No fake checkout, fake guarantee, fake booking system, or fake reviews. CTAs either use tel/mailto or link to the original live site.

## Prototype Sections
